This summer I had the opportunity to work with Dr. Eric Rawdon through the Center for Applied Mathematics Research Program. The purpose of the research was to study the structures of knots and links using two-dimensional grid projections.

 
 

In my CISC 230 class last fall, my favorite project centered around creating a kiosk for a coffee shop. When run, the program would allow the user to choose from black coffee, decaf, and tea. They could also add oat milk and a sugar syrup to their drink. When the user completed their order, the program calculated the total cost with the MN Sales Tax included. I love coffee, so this was extra applicable to my life.

 

I am currently working on a project for the Mind Matters Club. We would like for St. Thomas to supply each student with a membership to the Headspace app. The app contains numerous meditations for all sorts of moods, days, times, and goals. I plan to gather research of the benefits of meditation for mental health and the app's success to present in front of certain St. Thomas faculty.
